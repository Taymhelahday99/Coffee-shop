Did you know?
Coffee drinkers live longer than non-coffee users
According to Harvard Health Publishing, research has linked moderate intake (about 3 to 4 cups per day) to a longer life span, as well as a lower risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and Parkinson's disease.

Don't like Coffee?

You are probably one of the sweet-toothed people who thinks coffee is bitter,
you'd be right but the good news is that you don't have to take the dark coffee.
Here are various varieties of how to take your coffee, bet you would find one that suits your tastebuds

Frappucino

A Frappuccino is a blended coffee drink that is typically made with coffee or espresso, milk, and ice. It is blended together in a blender, which gives it a smooth and creamy texture. Additional ingredients such as syrups and whipped cream can also be added to customize the flavor.

Cappucino

Cappuccino, coffee drink made of equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and frothed milk(milk foam). Traditional baristas follow a 1:1:1 ratio of equal parts espresso,steamed milk, and milk foam when making cappuccinos, and may sometimes garnish the beverage with cinnamon powder or cocoa powder.

Check out the recipe

Latte

A latte is a traditional coffee drink that is made with espresso and steamed milk. It is typically served in a larger cup than a traditional espresso and it has a stronger coffee flavor. A latte also usually has a layer of foam on top, which adds a creamy texture to the drink.

Check out the recipe

Americano

An Americano is a classic espresso-style drink with a smooth and well-rounded flavor. It has the boldness of traditional coffee but without too much bitterness because the hot water tones down its taste. It is prepared by diluting an espresso shot with hot water at a 1:3 to 1:4 ratio, resulting in a drink that retains the complex flavors of espresso, but in a lighter way

Check out the recipe

Mocha

Mocha, also known as moccacino is a deliciously sweet, nutty and chocolatey coffee drink. It is made a shot of espresso is combined with a chocolate powder or syrup, followed by milk or cream. It is a variant of a latte, in the sense that it is often 1/3 espresso and 2/3 steamed milk. However, a chocolate flavour is added, and this can be milk or dark.
